# BNSF Mock Recruiter Call
**Recruiter:** Dinesh Valleru, Principal Technical Recruiter, BNSF Railway
**Role:** Product, Platform & Enterprise Front End Sr/Staff Software Engineer (Remote - US)
**Purpose:** Preparation script for recruiter screening call

---

## The Call

**Dinesh:** Hi Mark, thanks for making time today. I'm Dinesh, a Principal Technical Recruiter here at BNSF. How are you doing?

**Mark:** Doing well, thanks Dinesh. Appreciate you reaching out. I was excited to see the message.

**Dinesh:** Great! So I came across your profile and it looked like a strong match for our Sr/Staff Frontend Engineer role. I wanted to connect, learn a bit more about your background, and tell you about the position and next steps. Does that sound good?

**Mark:** Absolutely, that works for me.

---

### Tell Me About Yourself

**Dinesh:** Perfect. So tell me a little about yourself and what you're currently doing.

**Mark:** Sure. I'm a Senior Angular developer and frontend engineer based in Rancho Cucamonga, California. For the past eight years I've been with ClearCadence, a professional services firm specializing in Alfresco and Hyland content and process management platforms. In that role I've been the principal engineer on most of our client engagements, building production Angular applications for enterprise clients like Wells Fargo, Manulife, NASA, and the Department of Defense. The work I'm most proud of is architecting ClearView, an Angular platform I designed from the ground up that was adopted by multiple clients as their primary interface. It started as a project and became a product.

---

### Why Are You Looking?

**Dinesh:** That's impressive. What's prompting you to look at new opportunities now?

**Mark:** Honestly, I've gotten as far as I can go in a consulting context. I want to be part of a product engineering organization where I can own something long-term, work with a real team, mentor junior engineers, and have my technical decisions compound over time rather than reset with each engagement. BNSF's platform engineering mission specifically caught my attention. Building reusable, scalable software that enables other teams is exactly the kind of leverage-creating work I gravitate toward.

---

### Angular Experience

**Dinesh:** Can you tell me more about your experience with Angular specifically?

**Mark:** Angular has been my primary framework for eight-plus years. I've built everything from complex workflow-driven forms and dynamic task lists to full administrative dashboards and advanced search interfaces. I work daily in TypeScript and RxJS, and I've led major version migrations, most recently an Angular 14 to 17 upgrade at Wells Fargo. I also have experience with the broader ecosystem. Keycloak for authentication, Elasticsearch and Kibana for search and analytics, GraphQL APIs at Manulife, and full-stack Java Spring integrations when needed.

---

### Mobile / Cross-Platform Gap

**Dinesh:** The role also mentions mobile and cross-platform frameworks like React Native and Flutter. Do you have experience there?

**Mark:** That's an honest gap. My background has been web-focused rather than native mobile. That said, the TypeScript foundation and component architecture patterns transfer directly, and I'm comfortable ramping quickly on new frameworks. If mobile becomes a core part of the role I'd want to understand the balance between web and mobile responsibilities.

---

### Compensation

**Dinesh:** What are your compensation expectations?

**Mark:** I'm genuinely open to what BNSF determines is fair based on the level and scope of the role. I saw the posted range and it's competitive. I'd rather let the interview process inform the leveling conversation than anchor prematurely on a number.

---

### Leveling — Sr vs Staff

**Dinesh:** Speaking of leveling, the role is posted as Sr or Staff. How do you see yourself?

**Mark:** I'd say solidly Senior with real Staff tendencies, particularly around platform thinking and building tools that enable other engineers. I'll be honest though, my background is PSO consulting rather than a structured product org, so I'm genuinely open to where you see me fitting after the interview process. What matters most to me is the right scope and a clear growth path.

---

### Vacation Timing

**Dinesh:** I do see in your email that you have an international trip coming up. Can you tell me more about that?

**Mark:** Yes, I wanted to flag that proactively. I leave this Friday and I'm back in approximately two weeks. I'm fully committed to this process. I just want to make sure we're setting realistic expectations on timing so nothing gets rushed. Given that the anticipated start date is June 1st, I'm hoping there's enough runway to work around it.

**Dinesh:** That's not a problem at all, we appreciate you being upfront. We can schedule next steps for when you return. Does that work?

**Mark:** That works perfectly.

---

### Mark's Questions

**Dinesh:** Before I let you go, do you have any questions for me?

**Mark:** A few, yes.

- Which engineering track is this role sitting in — product, platform, or enterprise?
- What does the frontend tech stack look like today beyond Angular?
- What does the interview process look like from here? Is there a take-home component or is it all live sessions?
- How does BNSF define the difference between Senior and Staff in practice — is it scope of impact, people influence, or something else?

**Dinesh:** Good questions. The role can sit across teams depending on fit and we'll figure that out as we go. I'll send you details on the interview process by email after our call.

**Mark:** Perfect. I really appreciate the time Dinesh. I'm excited about this one.

**Dinesh:** Same here Mark, talk soon.

## What to Ask About Interview Format

Dinesh's email mentioned next steps but did not confirm the technical screen format. Ask directly:

> "Can you describe the format of the technical screen so I can prepare appropriately?"

The format is unknown and could be any of the following:
- Live coding session
- Take-home assignment
- Timed online assessment (HackerRank, etc.)
- Architecture whiteboard discussion
- Some combination of the above
